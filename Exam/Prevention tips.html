Guidelines on Facemasks

1. Make sure the face mask covers your mouth and nose, and fits snugly over your face.
2. Isuot ang mask sa lahat ng oras habang nasa pampublikong lugar, kahit habang nakikipag-usap. Tanggalin lamang ito kapag nasa bahay na o kailangang kumain.
3. Do not touch your face while wearing the mask
4. Wash your hands with soap and water or an alcohol-based solution before and after adjusting or touching your mask.

Mandatory Home Quarantine

1. Allocate a separate room with adequate ventilation at home if possible. Household members should stay in another room or should be separated from the returnee.
2. Maintain at least one meter distance from family members.
3. Other household members should use a separate bathroom. If sharing the same bathroom, cleaning of taps, doorknobs and utensils with soap and water are a requirement.
4. Minimize visitors to the house, the returnee should not interact with any visitors.
5. Frequent hand washing with soap and water for at least 20 seconds at a time and maintain alcohol based hand hygiene in instances where hand washing facilities are inadequate

Social Distancing

1. You should just wave when greeting friends.
2. You should just text, chat or telebabad instead of visiting relatives and friends.
3. Should not go to crowded places and unimportant gatherings.
4. Should not shake hands, hug or kiss especially people who are more at risk of contracting the virus such as the elderly (65 years and older) and those with weak resistance.
5. Always wash your hands for at least 20 seconds
6. Avoid touching your eyes, nose and mouth
7. Cough or sneeze inside the elbow
8. Avoid touching things that other people often touch

How to Make Disinfectant Bleach Solution

1. Gather all the materials you will need. Be sure to work in a well-ventilated, shaded, and air-protected area.
2. Wear personal protective equipment (gloves, goggles, aprons, and boots) while handling and preparing bleach solution.
3. In preparing the solution, pour the required amount of water into a plastic container and add the bleach (do not reverse this process).
4. To use household bleach (containing 5% active chlorine), mix 1 milliliter (mL) of bleach and 9 mL of water, or 100 mL of bleach and 900 mL of water
5. Label disinfectant containers along with chlorine concentration measurements.
6. Prepare a new bleach and water solution daily to maintain its effectiveness.
